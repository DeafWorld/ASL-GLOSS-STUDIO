What is ASL Gloss?

ASL gloss is a written system using English words to represent ASL signs and grammar structure.

📝 Written Representation

Gloss uses CAPITAL LETTERS to represent signs, with special notation for fingerspelling (fs-NAME), repetition (WORK++), and non-manual markers (eyebrows up).

🔄 Grammar Structure

Shows ASL's unique grammar: Time-Topic-Comment order, WH-questions at the end, and how facial expressions convey meaning.

🎓 Learning Tool

Perfect for students, educators, and performers to understand ASL sentence structure before signing.

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about ASL Gloss Studio

Can I use this for homework or assignments? ▼

Yes! ASL Gloss Studio is designed as a learning tool to help you understand ASL grammar structure. However, always verify glosses with your instructor, as different ASL programs may have slightly different notation preferences. Use it as a study aid to understand time-topic-comment structure, WH-question placement, and non-manual markers.

Is the gloss 100% accurate? ▼

The studio uses established ASL grammar rules and patterns, but glossing can vary based on context, regional differences, and signing style. Think of it as a helpful guide rather than the only "correct" way. Real ASL includes facial expressions, body shifts, and spatial grammar that written gloss can only partially capture. Always consider the full visual-spatial nature of ASL.

Do I need to know ASL before using this? ▼

No! Gloss is actually a great way to START learning ASL grammar. You don't need to know signs yet - the studio shows you how ASL sentence structure differs from English. However, to actually SIGN the gloss, you'll need to learn the individual signs. Use this alongside sign vocabulary learning for best results.

Can I suggest improvements or report errors? ▼

Absolutely! This tool continuously improves with community feedback. If you notice glossing that seems incorrect or have suggestions for better examples, reach out through the YouTube channel @Jared.Finney. Your input helps make this resource better for everyone in the ASL learning community.

What do the notations like (eyebrows up) and ++ mean? ▼

(eyebrows up/down) = non-manual markers (facial expressions that are grammatically required). ++ = repetition of a sign. CL: = classifier (handshapes representing objects/movement). fs- = fingerspelling. IX = indexing/pointing. These notations help capture the visual-spatial elements of ASL in written form.
